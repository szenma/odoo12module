User Guide

Main Configurations:

1-Setting up your 'Accounting Policy':

From Configurations, set up the 'Accounting Policy' to either 'One Account for All Employees' or 'Account Per Employee'. (BE WARNED! Changing between the two policies (while loan records are in place) may cause unwanted results, so be advised to study and choose the most suitable policy from day one.)

Don't forget that your accounts must be 'Reconiliable'. Each employee should have a related user but to avoid extra charges for new users, we recommend to deactivate the employees' related users.

2-Adding the 'Loans Salary Rule' to your applied 'Salary Structure'

You can configure the structure's 'Max Loan Percentage' which is the allowed percentage of the employee wage to be requested as a loan, taking into consideration the previous loans balance for the same employee.
You can also configure the 'Interest Rate' which indicates the interest rate to be added to the loan's amount.

3-Defining 'Loan Types':

Navigate to 'Loan Type' from 'Configurations' menu in 'Payroll' and fill in the needed details.

Loan Creation

Navigate to 'Loans' menu in 'Payroll' to create a new loan and fill in the needed details and don't forget to populate the 'Payments for Loan' table with the preferred settlement dates.


The 'Payment' smart button will appear once the loan is approved.

'Payment' smart button shows you the loan payments and it also reflects in the 'Accounting' module as a 'Vendor Payment'.

Create a new 'Employee Payslip' from Payroll, fill in the Employee details and their Loan Payment will be visible in a line under the 'Other Inputs' table.

After computing the sheet, you will be able to see the added 'Loans Salary Rule' in the payslip.

After the confirmation of the payslip, the loan amount allocated to this payslip's month will be deducted from the loan balance.

Also note that a 'Receipt Voucher' header button will appear once the loan is approved enabling you to create payments the employee pays in advance (non-payslip payments).


Once a 'Receipt Voucher' is created and confirmed, it will be listed under the new 'Receipts' smart button and will be reflected in the 'Accounting' module as a 'Customer Payment'. Notice that the payment lines in the 'Payments for Loan' table are checked as deducted once the 'Receipt Voucher' is confirmed.
